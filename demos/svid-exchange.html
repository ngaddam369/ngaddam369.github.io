svid-exchange interactive

A Zero Trust service that trades a SPIFFE SVID (presented over mTLS) for a scoped, short-lived ES256 JWT — so microservices authenticate each other with cryptographic workload identity instead of static shared secrets.

Real ES256 tokens, signed in your browser with WebCrypto — copy any token into jwt.io and it verifies.
⚠ WebCrypto needs a secure context. Open this page over https (GitHub Pages) or http://localhost — opening the file directly (file://) may disable token signing.

How one exchange works

Watch a single exchange end-to-end: a caller service proves its identity with an SVID, the exchange service applies policy and mints a token, and the target service verifies that token offline. Click Play, or step through it.

    Mint your own token

    Pick a caller, a target, the scopes it asks for, and a TTL. The real policy engine decides what's granted; a real ES256 JWT is signed in your browser. Then verify it, watch it expire, revoke it, replay it, or tamper with it.

    Defined policies config/policy.example.yaml

    caller → targetallowed scopesmax ttl
    These three pairs are the only ones that succeed (click a row to load it). Any other caller→target pair has no policy → denied. A matching pair still fails if none of the requested scopes are in its allowed set.
